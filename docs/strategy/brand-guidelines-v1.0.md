## Typography

### Primary Typeface

#### For Headlines & Display
**Font Family:** Playfair Display (Serif)
- **Weights:** 400 (Regular), 700 (Bold)
- **Usage:** Book titles, main headlines, hero text
- **Import:** `@import url('https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Playfair+Display:wght@400;700&display=swap');`
- **Rationale:** Elegant, literary, classic serif that reflects book publishing heritage

#### For Body Text
**Font Family:** Lora (Serif) or Merriweather (Serif)
- **Weights:** 400 (Regular), 700 (Bold)
- **Usage:** Body text, descriptions, book content
- **Import:** `@import url('https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Lora:wght@400;700&display=swap');`
- **Rationale:** Highly readable serif, optimized for longer reading (important for older target audience)

#### Alternative: Sans-Serif Option
**Font Family:** Inter or Source Sans Pro
- **Weights:** 400 (Regular), 500 (Medium), 600 (Semi-Bold)
- **Usage:** UI elements, navigation, buttons, metadata
- **Rationale:** Clean, modern, accessible for digital interfaces

### Typography Scale

```css
/* Typography Scale */
h1: font-size: 2.5rem (40px), font-weight: 700, line-height: 1.2
h2: font-size: 2rem (32px), font-weight: 700, line-height: 1.3
h3: font-size: 1.5rem (24px), font-weight: 700, line-height: 1.4
h4: font-size: 1.25rem (20px), font-weight: 500, line-height: 1.4
h5: font-size: 1.125rem (18px), font-weight: 500, line-height: 1.5
h6: font-size: 1rem (16px), font-weight: 500, line-height: 1.5

/* Body Text - Optimized for Older Readers */
body: font-size: 1.125rem (18px), font-weight: 400, line-height: 1.8
small: font-size: 1rem (16px), font-weight: 400, line-height: 1.6
caption: font-size: 0.875rem (14px), font-weight: 400, line-height: 1.5
```

### Typography Guidelines

#### Readability (Critical for Older Audience)
- **Minimum Body Text:** 18px (16px absolute minimum)
- **Line Height:** 1.6-1.8 for body text (generous spacing)
- **Line Length:** 60-75 characters per line (optimal reading width)
- **Letter Spacing:** Normal (no tight tracking)
- **Word Spacing:** Comfortable (no condensed fonts)

#### Hierarchy
1. **H1:** Book titles, main page headlines
2. **H2:** Section headers, chapter titles
3. **H3:** Subsection headers
4. **Body:** All reading content
5. **Small:** Metadata, captions, fine print

## Imagery & Photography

### Style Guidelines

#### Book Cover Photography
- **Style:** High-quality, professional book cover images
- **Format:** High-resolution (300dpi minimum for print)
- **Composition:** Clean, centered, well-lit
- **Background:** Neutral or complementary to cover design
- **Consistency:** Uniform lighting and presentation across all covers

#### Author Photos
- **Style:** Professional, approachable, authentic
- **Composition:** Head and shoulders, natural lighting
- **Background:** Neutral or subtle (moss green tones work well)
- **Expression:** Warm, welcoming, literary
- **Format:** Square or portrait orientation

#### Location Photography
- **Style:** Scottish landscapes, Highland scenes, cultural heritage
- **Mood:** Atmospheric, evocative, literary
- **Composition:** Thoughtful, not overly dramatic
- **Color Treatment:** Natural tones with slight moss green accent
- **Subjects:** Highland landscapes, Tay swimming spots, Scottish culture

### Image Do's & Don'ts

✅ **Do:**
- Use high-quality, professional imagery
- Maintain consistent style across all images
- Feature real Scottish locations and culture
- Show book covers prominently and clearly
- Use natural, authentic photography

❌ **Don't:**
- Use generic stock photos
- Over-filter or heavily edit
- Mix inconsistent styles
- Use low-resolution images
- Clutter images with text overlays

## Tone of Voice

### Brand Voice Characteristics

1. **Literary & Thoughtful**
   - Use rich, descriptive language
   - Show genuine appreciation for literature
   - Reflect depth and consideration
   - Example: "We're delighted to bring Neil Gunn's 'The Grey Coast' back to readers in this centenary edition."

2. **Authentic & Genuine**
   - Write from the heart, not marketing speak
   - Be honest about our mission (break even, not profit)
   - Show real passion for books
   - Example: "We publish books we love, not just books that sell."

3. **Welcoming & Accessible**
   - Avoid literary pretension
   - Make literature approachable
   - Invite readers into the conversation
   - Example: "Whether you're discovering Neil Gunn for the first time or returning to a favorite, we're here to share these stories."

4. **Heritage-Focused**
   - Celebrate Scottish culture respectfully
   - Honor literary traditions
   - Connect past and present
   - Example: "Preserving Scotland's literary heritage, one book at a time."

5. **Passionate & Enthusiastic**
   - Show genuine excitement about books
   - Share the joy of discovery
   - Invite readers to share our passion
   - Example: "We're thrilled to launch with five exceptional titles that showcase the richness of Scottish literature."

### Writing Guidelines

- **Use UK English** spelling throughout
- **Keep sentences clear** and well-structured
- **Write in active voice** where possible
- **Be authentic** - write as you would speak to a friend
- **Avoid jargon** - literary terms are fine, but explain when needed
- **Show, don't tell** - let the books speak for themselves
- **Respect the reader** - assume intelligence, not expertise

### Examples

**Good (On-Brand):**
> "At North House Press, we believe great books deserve to be published, regardless of commercial viability. Our launch list celebrates Scottish culture through both new voices and forgotten classics, including Neil Gunn's 'The Grey Coast' in a special centenary edition."

**Bad (Off-Brand):**
> "North House Press is a cutting-edge publishing company that leverages innovative distribution strategies to maximize market penetration and optimize reader engagement through data-driven content curation."

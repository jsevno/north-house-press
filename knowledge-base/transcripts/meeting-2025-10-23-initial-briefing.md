# Meeting Transcript: Initial Project Briefing

**Meeting Title:** North House Press website project with David

**Participants:** David Warwick Graham (North House Press), Jonny Scott (Developer)

---

## Meeting Summary

Initial briefing call to discuss North House Press website development. David outlined the company mission, initial book list, and website requirements. Discussion covered technical approach, e-commerce needs, and project scope.

---

## Key Discussion Points

### Company Background
- **Mission:** Publish books we love, break even (not profit-focused)
- **Launch:** May 2025 with 5 books initially
- **Focus:** Scottish culture, mix of new titles and lost classics
- **Directors:** David Warwick Graham & Kirsty Jane Gunn
- **Location:** The Grey House, Dalreavoch, Rogart, Sutherland, Scotland

### Initial Book List
1. **Neil Gunn Revival (3 books):**
   - "The Grey Coast" (1926 debut) - centenary PR hook
   - Originally published by Jonathan Cape, later Faber & Faber
   - 20 novels total, all Highland-based, largely forgotten
2. **New Title:** "A Year In Water" by Beth McDonough (Dundee poet/wild swimmer)
   - Practical tips, poems about wild swimming through calendar year
   - Focus on Tay swimming spots
3. **Autumn:** Additional nonfiction book

### Website Requirements
- **Architecture:** 6 pages (Home, Books, Authors, News/Events, Contacts, Submissions)
- **Shopping Cart:** For print + ebooks (UK/Ireland only)
- **Direct Fulfillment:** To avoid bookshop margins (50%+)
- **Color Scheme:** Moss green (from existing logo/branding)
- **Assets Available:** High-res covers, logo, letterhead

### Technical Considerations
- **Platform:** WordPress most common in publishing, but agnostic
- **Ebook Integration:** Glassbox (20% commission) - David's previous experience
- **Target Audience:** Older readers (demographic consideration)
- **Simplicity:** Neither David nor Kirsty technically minded - needs to be user-friendly

### Business Context
- **David's Background:** Former IPG (Independent Publisher's Guild) board chair
  - Represents ~650 independent publishers
  - AI conferences twice yearly
  - Potential speaking opportunities for Jonny
- **George Weekly:** Runs AI training courses for publishers (former Hachette digital director)

### Project Approach
- **Initial Phase:** MVP pro bono
- **Payment:** Discussion for future development phases
- **Compensation:** Book copies as partial payment (early 2025)
- **Scope:** Keep it simple, avoid over-engineering
